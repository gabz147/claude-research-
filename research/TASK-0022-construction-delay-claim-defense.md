# TASK-0022 — Construction delay / change-order claim-defense documentation (OPP-006)

> Net-new discovery cycle. Seeded candidate (NEXT_ACTION / ADR-015 doctrine). Verdict: **64 → ARCHIVE** (third Archived row). Killed at the **both-sides named-incumbent vacuum screen** BEFORE deep research, exactly as the ADR-015 doctrine prescribes.

## Step 1 — Frame
**Falsifiable question:** Is there an *un-productized* vacuum for a contested-defense / record-reconstruction artifact that assembles the schedule + RFI + daily-log + change-order trail needed to win or defend a construction **delay / change-order claim** — a high-ticket B2B buyer (GC / sub / owner / their counsel) with **no dominant productized owner on either the prevention OR the recovery side**?

**Seeded hypothesis (from NEXT_ACTION):** fragmented, embarrassing, un-named-budget pain served *only* by a services-model claims-consultant industry (delay-analysis experts), with **no productized owner**. → *This hypothesis must be verified affirmatively, not assumed (the OPP-003/004/005 lesson).*

**Prior:** LIKELY this caps like the prior 5 — construction claims is a large, mature, named-money-pool industry (delay damages, liquidated damages, $177.5B/yr U.S. non-productive-time stat), which by ADR-015 is precisely the profile that has *already recruited incumbents on both sides*.

### The specific artifact none of them sell?
The hypothesis needed a *contested-defense record-assembly product with no owner*. There isn't one:
- **Capture side** is owned by Procore (+ P6) and now Procore's litigation-grade AI agents.
- **Forensic/court-ready side** is owned by **SmartPM** (a product, not just services) plus a deep consultant bench.
- **EOT-claim assembly** is owned by WeBuild/Flexbase/Opteam.
No defensible gap remains between these. The seeded "services-only, no product" premise is **FALSIFIED affirmatively on both sides** — SmartPM alone closes it.

## Step 4 — Disprove (the bull case, then why it fails)
- *Bull:* claims are still painful, expensive, and many mid-market subs document poorly → reconstruction demand persists.
- *Why it fails:* pain ≠ vacuum. The buyer with budget (GCs/owners/counsel on large claims) is already served by SmartPM + forensic firms; the mid-market sub with poor docs has *low WTP* and is being captured up-funnel by Procore's free-ish AI daily-log/RFI agents. Both the high-WTP and low-WTP ends are claimed. No durable wedge for a new entrant; AI is already the incumbents' headline feature, not our edge.

## Step 5 — Economics
Real and large (delay/disruption claims routinely $1M–$100M+; liquidated damages; $177.5B/yr U.S. non-productive time). **This is the tell, not the opportunity** (ADR-015): a headline named-money-pool with court-enforceable dispute rights is exactly the profile that recruited SmartPM, Procore-AI, and an entire forensic industry.

## Conclusion
- **Supporting (for the problem):** large, recurring, high-stakes, deep-pocket B2B pain with court-enforceable dispute rights.
- **Contradicting (against the opportunity):** both wedges productized + a mature forensic-services industry; AI is the incumbents' feature; no un-owned artifact; mid-market low-WTP being captured up-funnel by Procore.
- **Final assessment:** **64? No — 58 → ARCHIVE.** Killed at the vacuum screen. **6th consecutive cap.**
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (vacuum verdict is HIGH-confidence — named productized incumbents on both sides; exact $-quantum of the addressable wedge not separately validated, hence not LOW-but-not-HIGH).
