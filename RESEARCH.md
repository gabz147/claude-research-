## Open questions
- **(ADR-019, refines ADR-018) An owned-position play that passes the strip-out-the-AI screen STILL needs three more things — what niche has all of them?** OPP-008 (Li-ion/BESS MGA) was the first candidate to clear the ADR-018 strip-out screen (a genuine owned position) and **still capped (58)** for three *new* reasons: (a) the vacuum was a **transient hard-market cycle**, not structural (NARDAC filled BESS in <2 yrs; market softening into 2026); (b) the moat (proprietary loss data) was a **cold-start** that accrues only to the incumbent; (c) an MGA needs **regulated binding authority/capital** (un-buildable by a small org). **Next-candidate profile:** an owned-position play whose moat is **originable from zero** (network/workflow lock-in that compounds from the FIRST customers, not after years), in a **structurally** (not cyclically) unserved niche, **buildable without a regulated charter or carrier capacity.**
- **(ADR-018, the lead screen — still in force) What does an OWNED, AI-powered (not AI-IS-the-product) opportunity look like for us?** All 7 documentation-archetype picks capped because "AI reads/assembles/comprehends/documents an artifact corpus → a defensible record" is commoditized by horizontal AI AND absorbed for ~free by whoever owns the artifact's repository. **FIRST screen (before the vacuum check):** "strip out the AI — is there still a business (a workflow I own, a transaction I sit in, data only I have, a channel only I reach)?" If the product IS the AI reading documents, reject it. (OPP-008 passed this screen — the screen works; ADR-019 adds the *next* gate.)
