# MARKET ANALYSIS — 01-ira-audit-defense
**Date:** 2026-06-16  **Confidence:** MED

## Market definition (who/what, boundaries)
Defense, remediation, and recapture-mitigation services for **already-claimed IRA PWA-enhanced clean-energy tax credits** (PTC/ITC/45Y/48E and related), 2023–2027 vintages, when those credits face IRS examination, the Form 7220 correction/penalty regime, or §48 recapture. Boundary: this is the **post-claim / contested** market — distinct from *preventive* compliance (greenfield payroll/ratio tracking), which is a separate, already-contested software market (LCPtracker, DSPTCH, et al.). Adjacencies that share the same engine: **45Q** (survives OBBBA), **Davis-Bacon** federal-contract audits (perennial), **FEOC** clawbacks (post-2025).

## Size — TAM / SAM / SOM (numbers + sources + math)
- **TAM (credit value at risk):** PWA satisfaction yields a **5× credit multiplier**; the 2023–2027 vintage of PWA-claimed clean-energy credits represents **multi-$B of enhanced credit value** exposed to recapture/penalty. Penalty mechanics alone: **$5,000/worker** (×2 for intentional disregard) + back-wage + interest per failure (FACT, Form 7220). A single utility-scale project can carry hundreds of laborers → six- to seven-figure penalty exposure per project. — *math illustrative; precise vintage credit totals = UNKNOWN, no clean public aggregate.*
- **SAM (serviceable — defense/remediation spend):** the slice of that exposure spent on advisory/legal defense + remediation. **UNKNOWN in $**; today captured by Big-4 + tax/construction law firms at bespoke rates.
- **SOM:** **UNKNOWN** — gated by WTP for a software-enabled vendor (see CUSTOMER_ANALYSIS) and by channel choice (sell-to-firms vs direct).
- ⚠️ All three figures are **directionally LIKELY-large but quantitatively UNKNOWN**; no published market-sizing exists. This is itself a finding: sizing requires primary data.

## Growth & trends (tailwinds, headwinds)
- **Tailwind (FACT):** IRS built dedicated PWA enforcement instruments — **Form 7220** (Dec 2025, attest/correct/penalty) and **Form 4255** (penalty/recapture reporting). Enforcement is institutionalizing.
- **Tailwind (LIKELY):** OBBBA's compressed beginning-of-construction deadlines triggered a 2025–2026 "rush to start," *enlarging* the vintage base that enters audit windows through ~2030.
- **Tailwind (FACT):** §48/§48E recapture rules still unfinalized → dispute volume.
- **Headwind (FACT):** IRS enforcement funding cut **$45.6B → $24B**; TIGTA staffing cuts — fewer human examiners may suppress the audit *rate* (offset partly by self-attestation/document-matching).
- **Headwind (FACT/structural):** **finite runoff** — IRA PWA credit *generation* phases out (solar/wind ITC by 2027; 45X 2030–32), so the core vintage base decays.
- **Headwind (emerging):** Form 7220 standardization risks **commoditizing** routine remediation into self-service.

## Fragmentation & structure
Highly fragmented on the supply side (many Big-4 + regional tax/construction law firms, manual delivery); concentrated, sophisticated demand side (developers, EPCs, tax-equity investors, **credit transferees**). Fragmentation + manual delivery = the productization opening; concentration of buyers = favorable for a **channel/enterprise** motion over long-tail SMB.

## Buying dynamics
Event-driven (audit notice / recapture trigger), not subscription-native per buyer. Decision makers: tax director / VP tax / CFO (developer side); engagement partner (firm side). Budget = controversy/legal line, low price-sensitivity when multi-$M credit at stake (LIKELY). Sales motion likely **enterprise / firm-channel**, not self-serve.

## Timing — why now
2023–2024 vintages are hitting audit/recapture windows **now**; Form 7220 first applies **TY2025**; recapture rules still settling. The window to build the **audit-outcome data moat** is the next ~2–4 years, before the vintage base decays and before preventive incumbents extend downstream.
