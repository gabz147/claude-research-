# Anti-Slop

Make the org's writing read like a sharp human analyst, not an LLM. Apply before posting any report, finding, or outreach.

## Core rules
1. **Cut throat-clearing openers.** State the point directly. Delete: "Here's the thing/what/why…", "It turns out", "The truth is", "Let me be clear", "Make no mistake", "The uncomfortable truth is", "I'll be honest".
2. **Delete emphasis crutches.** "Full stop." · "Period." · "Let that sink in." · "This matters because" · "Here's why that matters."
3. **Break formulaic structures** (false drama — state Y directly instead):
   - "Not X. It's Y." / "X isn't the problem. Y is." / "The question isn't X, it's Y."
   - "not just X but also Y" · "is about X but not Y" · "stops being X and starts being Y"
   - Dramatic one-line fragments for effect. Rhetorical-question setups.
   - Negative listings ("This isn't about A, B, or C…").
4. **Active voice, human subjects.** No passive constructions. No inanimate agency ("the data reveals", "the complaint becomes a fix"). A person or named actor does the thing.
5. **Kill adverbs and intensifiers.** "very, really, incredibly, fundamentally, significantly" — usually deletable.
6. **Plain words over jargon.** navigate→handle · unpack→explain · leverage→use · utilize→use · robust→solid · delve→look at.
7. **No em-dash-driven dramatic asides as a tic.** Use them sparingly, like a human editor would.

## How to apply
After writing any prose artifact: re-read once, run this checklist, cut. If a sentence survives only because it sounds profound, delete it. Numbers and evidence over adjectives — this org's claims are tagged FACT/LIKELY/SPECULATION/UNKNOWN; let the tag and the source carry the weight, not the prose.

## Exclusions
Do not "de-slop" code, JSON, file contracts, or quoted source material. Apply only to human-facing prose.
